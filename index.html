Pipes

2023.01.04

What is a |? The | character, also known as the vertical bar, was used to denote a period or full stop across Europe and India. The pipe as we know it today in programming was originally represented with a broken vertical bar ¦ to avoid confusion with I and l in early fonts without serifs. ISO/IEC 8859-1 introduced separate | and ¦ character, with the former recognized as the canonical vertical bar and pipe.

The word pipe itself is almost unchanged from the vulgar Latin pipa. Pipa referred to a tube-shaped instrument that could pipare, or chirp and peep. This word for a chirping, peeping instrument an also became a word for an instrument of conveyance because of the overlap in the form of the function. A tubular reed made for a good musical pipe; a ceramic tube made for a good fluid pipe.

In Unix-based systems, piping is a way of passing messages between programs. One process' output, or standard output stream stdout, is piped directly into another process' input, or standard input stream stdin. What is so standard about this streams? They adhere to a common format, such as text or binary data. The operating system provides an in-memory buffer to receive the output program's stream until its process is complete or the buffer is full, at which point the input program is prompted to read from the buffer. The Unix shell syntax for this looks like program1 | program2. A real, practical example looks like ls -c | head. This pipeline lists the ten most recently modified files in the present working directory.

We can thank Douglas McIlroy for developing the concept and pushing for its inclusion into the original UNIX operating system.
